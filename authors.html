Submission criteria and process

Schedule and types of submission

We accept submissions on a rolling basis throughout the year. Every fortnight, we publish manuscripts and culminate them into an issue at the end of the semester term.

Due to the volume of submissions we receive, there is no guarantee that your submission will be considered for the forthcoming issue. As we process submissions in the order received, your submission might instead be considered for the following issue.

We accept primary research articles as well literature reviews from all academic disciplines including the natural and social sciences, humanities, commerce, and engineering.

Submission criteria

Articles submitted to CJUR cannot have been previously published or accepted to an additional peer-reviewed journal or book (print or electronic). By submitting an article to CJUR, the author must acknowledge that the material is not also currently under review at another publication and that the material will not be submitted to another journal until the editorial board of CJUR has reached a decision. CJUR does accept articles from coursework but this must be disclosed at the time of submission. Written acknowledgements must also be made to the institution and course for which it was originally intended, and the author must confirm that the article has already been graded prior to submission.

You must register to OJS in order to make a submission

Making a submission

To submit to CJUR, simply register as an author on our online submission system to submit your article.

Presubmission inquiries

If you have any concern regarding the eligibility of your article, please contact us.

Formatting

It is mandatory for all students to submit their manuscript in this submission template to ensure proper formatting when submitting to CJUR. You can consult the authors' guide and this submission template for more information. When submitting to CJUR, we ask that you:

  • Submit using Microsoft word (.doc or .docx file format)
  • Do not exceed a 3000 word limit (not including references and figure captions)
  • Ensure your abstract is between 150-300 words.
  • Use size 12 Times New Roman
  • Include figure captions at the end of the word document
  • Use APA style and inline citations only
  • List the names, affiliations, and e-mail addresses of two potential reviewers who are not involved with the project
  • Include a minimum of 10 references
  • Number every line on your manuscript.
  • Upload figures and illustrations separately from your text in lossless format (ex.: PNG, TIFF, GIF, BMP, etc.) and in a resolution of at least 300 dpi. In addition, all the datasets used to create figures must also be uploaded as a clearly labelled spreadsheet file.

    • The editorial board reserves the right to request further information from authors or changes in formatting before deciding whether a submission is to be accepted. Additional questions about formatting or submitting an article should be directed to info@cjur.ca.

    Editorial process

    Our editorial board consists of undergraduate students who screen the submissions to be considered for publication for adherence to guidelines and general fitness within the journal. Submissions are then reviewed by 1-2 faculty or graduate students who evaluate the quality and rigour of the article before they are accepted for publication in CJUR. Following review, the reviewer(s) and editorial board will declare each submission as being either: “accepted”, “accepted with minor revisions” or “resubmit for review.” Those whose submissions require minor revisions will have 2-3 weeks to return their submission with the appropriate amendments prior to publication.
